Statement of Purpose

"I believe the time has come for a leadership that is not only committed to representing students but also committed to transforming challenges into systems."

My administration is built on 6 pillars of transformation for UMYU.

1. Student Welfare & Support

  • Establish a fully functional Students' Help & Support Centre (physical & digital).
  • Create emergency response channels for health, finance, and crisis situations.
  • Improve hostel conditions, water supply, and transport services.
  • Introduce exam support: reading materials and night-class safety.

2. Academic Excellence

  • Academic Improvement Workshops for complex courses.
  • Mentorship programs pairing students with seniors and alumni.
  • Skill acquisition in digital tech, leadership, and entrepreneurship.
  • Partner with organizations for internships and scholarships.
  • Launch a “Reading Culture Revival Initiative”.

3. Safer & Inclusive Campus

  • Strengthen security through dialogue and safety committees.
  • Advocate for street lighting at lecture halls and hostels.
  • Establish a Gender-Sensitive Desk for discreet reporting.
  • Advocate for ramps and policies for students with disabilities.

4. Engagement & Leadership

  • Revive clubs and support student-led initiatives.
  • Leadership training for Class Reps and association heads.
  • Organize debates, talent shows, sports, and tech exhibitions.
  • Establish a Student Innovation & Creativity Lab.

5. Transparent Governance

  • Publish quarterly SUG activity and financial reports.
  • Open-door policy for ideas and complaints.
  • Hold town-hall meetings twice every semester.
  • Ensure inclusive decision-making across all faculties.

6. Management Collaboration

  • Maintain healthy, progressive dialogue with university management.
  • Advocate for student-friendly policies and timely resolutions.
  • Strengthen partnerships with Security, Academics, ICT, and Student Affairs units.

"Together, we can build the UMYU we dream of."
